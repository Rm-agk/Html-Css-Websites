Emirates Stadium

This is the inside of the emirates stadium which is home to Arsenal. It has a capacity of 60,000 seats and cost £390 million to construct. This stadium was built in 2004. This is where the present Arsenal team play their fixtures.

Highbury Stadium

This was Arsenal's previous stadium before the move to the emirates stadium. Once nicknamed the 'Home of Football', Highbury Stadium, still stands proud in Highbury, North London. It had a capacity of 38,000 seats and was where Arsenal's "Invincibles" played their games.

The change to the Emirates

Arsenal’s match day revenue increased from £37.4m in their old Highbury stadium to over £90m in the new Emirates Stadium (equivalent to an additional income of over £1m per match), and that excludes other commercial revenue; converting Highbury to residential units delivered £157m in 2011 alone. This financial success meant Arsenal could clear all their bank loans in 2010
